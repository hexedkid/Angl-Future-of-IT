Topic 3: Quantum Computing and Hyperspeed

Unlocking New Computational Power

**Quantum Computing** is a radically different approach to computation that harnesses the strange and powerful laws of quantum mechanics. Unlike classical computers that use **bits** (which are either 0 or 1), quantum computers use **qubits** (quantum bits).

A qubit can exist in a **superposition** of both 0 and 1 simultaneously, and multiple qubits can be **entangled**. This allows a quantum computer to explore millions of possibilities concurrently, giving it the potential to solve certain types of complex problems exponentially faster than any supercomputer today.

What Problems Can Quantum Computers Solve?

  1. **Drug and Material Discovery:** Simulating molecular interactions with perfect accuracy, accelerating the creation of new medicines and materials.
  2. **Financial Modeling:** Creating highly complex risk models that account for countless variables in real time.
  3. **Cryptography:** Posing a threat to current encryption standards, driving the need for new **quantum-resistant** security protocols.
