Topic 2: The Core of the Future — AI and Machine Learning

Decoding AI: More Than Just Robots

**Artificial Intelligence (AI)** is the ability of a computer system to simulate human intelligence to perform tasks such as visual perception, speech recognition, decision-making, and translation. At its heart lies **Machine Learning (ML)**, which is a subfield of AI that gives computers the ability to learn without being explicitly programmed.

Essentially, ML systems use vast amounts of data to **train algorithms** to recognize patterns and make predictions or decisions. The rapid advancements we see today are driven primarily by **Deep Learning (DL)**, which uses neural networks with multiple layers (hence "deep") to analyze complex data like images, sound, and text.

Key ML Paradigms:

  • **Supervised Learning:** Training with labeled data to predict outcomes (e.g., classifying emails as spam or not spam).
  • **Unsupervised Learning:** Discovering hidden patterns and structures in unlabeled data (e.g., customer segmentation).
  • **Generative AI:** The newest frontier, focusing on creating new content, such as text (GPT models), images (Midjourney), and even code.
